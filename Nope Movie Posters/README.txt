These three works were made for my Digital Graphics class in high school. We were tasked with creating posters for a movie, game, tv show, etc. I chose Nope, as it is my all-time favorite movie that you should definetly watch no pressure.

Eyes: Jean Jacket, despite being a cosmic alien horror, is still a wild animal that can be provoked through seemingly insignificant actions. For example, many wild animals can feel threatened or challenged by direct eye contact; Jean Jacket is no different. Because OJ has experience with animals, he quickly figures out how to fly under Jean Jacket's radar: by not looking straight at it. This detail is shown through the multitude of eyes looking straight up while one is looking down.

Flag Streamer: For a good chunk of the movie, Jean Jacket has a plastic horse wrapped in a line of flag streamers lodged in its throat. During that time, Jean Jacket's presence is hinted at with a line of colorful flags hanging from the sky. I chose to depict it in this design because it is easily recognizable to someone who knows what the flags represent. People who have not seen *Nope* merely see a line of flags, while those who have know that Jean Jacket is lurking not far above.

Shoe: An ever-present motif throughout the movie is the existence of bad miracles. During a flashback, we are shown exaclty what a bad miracle can be. The flashback takes place during the infamous Gordy's Home Massacre, in which one of the chimpanzees that played the role of Gordy was set off by a balloon popping and began to lash out violently. Jupe hid under the dining room table and witnessed it all. During the massacre, Jupe focuses on a shoe impossibly balanced on its sole. This design makes reference to that very shoe, complete with the stain of blood. The signficiance of the design stems from the motif of bad miracles, as the balanced shoe is a clear sign of the motif's presence during the scene.
